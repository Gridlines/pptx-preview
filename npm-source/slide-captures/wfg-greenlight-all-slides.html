Industry Overview

■The timeshare industry began in the 1970’s when several resort companies in the Florida market began offering right-to-use plans, or vacation licenses

■Overtime these evolved to deeded ownership contracts with a point system being added in 1991, which is the industry norm today

■Currently, an estimated 10MM U.S. households own a time share, with the industry expected to grow by ~7% each year from 2023 to 2028

■Industry saw a steep drop in demand due to covid travel restrictions not allowing current or potential customers to visit timeshares, with demand returning to pre- covid levels following the lifting of restrictions

(~$10bn of sales in 2022 v. ~$5bn in 2020)

Company Background

■Founded in 2011 and headquartered in Nashville, TN, Wesley Financial Group, (“WFG”, “Wesley”, or the “Company”) is the largest timeshare exit company in the U.S. (~6x larger than the next competitor)

■Since 2011, WFG has eliminated over $400MM in consumer timeshare debt and has helped release over 35K families from their timeshare

■Following the GFC, Chuck Mc Dowell (CEO & Owner), who previously worked as a timeshare salesman, founded WFG after seeing firsthand the coercive and misleading sales tactics that manipulate customers into purchasing a timeshare

■WFG employees use their familiarity with the timeshare process to negotiate directly with timeshare companies or to guide customers in their conversations with timeshares as they seek to exit

■Unlike other time share exit companies, Wesley does not attempt to sell, resell, or rent timeshares, they solely work to get customers out of their timeshares by demonstrating to timeshare companies that their sales team used coercive or misleading tactics to lock someone into a time share

•Generally, the key to releasing a customer from a timeshare is to prove the sales staff made deceptive, false, or misleading claims. Timeshares companies don’t want to release customers (and don’t have a team to facilitate exits in-house) but they acknowledge in these cases a release is justified and rely on WFG to bring customers with legitimate claims

■Timeshares are highly predatory, costing consumers ~$22K upfront, ~$4-5K a year, and ~$1K in annual maintenance fees, which grow ~4-6% a year. Most have inheritance clauses, passing them on to a family member in perpetuity

■WFG does not employ an outbound call center and does not cold call potential clients. They instead leverage Facebook (48% of YTD Nov-23 Leads), Radio & TV, (13%), Google (13%), and TikTok (6%) to advertise their service which brings them ~3,300 calls per week, earning an average ~5.0x Rev/Ad. Spend across all platforms

■The Company manages the entire process in house, which takes 14 months on avg. Customers receive a full refund if their case is not resolved within 36 months (current success rate of 98%)

•After winning a lawsuit brought by Wyndham alleging tortious interference and disclosure of trade secrets, WFG became Wyndham's “preferred partner” for timeshare exits. WFG has an exclusive agreement with Wyndham, helping to shorten the exit process to 4-months

•WFG currently has two outstanding lawsuits brought by two other timeshare companies with similar claims. $12.0MM of sources will be held in escrow to pay settlements in the event WFG loses. If the $12.0MM does not cover the settlement completely, the Seller will indemnify the remainder. The Company believes it will win these, as it did with the Wyndham suit, and that it will result in two additional preferred partner designations (a difficult status to obtain as relationships between exit firms and timeshares are adversarial yet symbiotic)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Process Overview

■After generating leads through social media, radio, and other advertising, prospective customers speak with a qualifications expert to determine if WFG can get them out of their timeshare

■Customers are asked to provide an extensive account of their purchasing process, outlining the communication they had with timeshare representative, providing written or verbal statements as possible

■If the Company believes they are able to help the customer, they will send them the Enrollment Agreement, prompting initial payment

■There are often 5-6 calls between first contact and signing the Enrollment Agreement, a process the sponsor believes the company will be able to condense into 2-3 calls

Lead Generation: Using social media, radio, and TV ads the company generates its inbound calls (~162,000 in FY22 and ~159,000 YTD Nov-23)

Customer Qualification: Potential customers then speak to a qualification specialist to determine if they fit WFGs criteria for enrollment (52% of inbound leads become qualified leas)

Customer Enrollment: Qualified leads then speak to a VP of Sales who walks them through the process and has them sign WFG’s Enrollment Agreement, bringing in initial payment (~35% of in bound calls end up speaking to a VP)

Resolution Department: Customer then moves to the Resolution department to work with them and the timeshare company towards a successful exit

Closing Department: As process moves forward, customer is transferred to the closing department to finalize any outstanding paper work and ultimately get the customer out of their time share (~7% of inbound leads go through the entire process and get out of their timeshare vs. 5% in 2022)

Investment Risks

■100% Project Based Revenue: WFG charges a fixed fee of ~$8.5-9K

■Reliance on Timeshare Industry: 35% decline in time share sales in 2009 and WFG had ~20% decline in revenue during the Covid pandemic

■Marketing or Processing Head Count Bloat: Potential for upside down LTV/ CAC or low utilization

•Mitigant: Most sales or processing employees are paid via a variable structure. FTEs were reduced from 500+ to ~280 to adjust for normalizing demand. Deal team believes cost structure is largely variable (headcount)

■Legal Risk: Pending lawsuits from timeshare sellers who allege i) WFG has provided legal advice to customers (without being licensed to practice law) and ii) they share industry secrets, resulting in damages to Diamond and Westgate

•Mitigant: WFG’s agreement with Wyndham arose from a lawsuit of the same kind as the current two. Company expects these to end with similar agreements

•Mitigant: $12.0MM to be held in escrow to fund any settlements 65% vs. 35% by the seller up to $12.0MM. Seller to indemnify WFG for any amounts above $12.0MM

Investment Merits

■Strong Value Proposition: Timeshares are burdensome to consumers ($22K upfront, ~$4-5K a year, plus ~$1K of annual maintenance fees which increase by ~4-6% each year). Most timeshares have inheritance clauses and are difficult to get out of without declaring personal bankruptcy. WFG can release customers for a flat fee of $8.5-9K

■Dominant Industry Position: 6x larger than the next largest player in the space

■Preferred Partner Status: Company has an agreement with Wyndham, one of the largest timeshare sellers, to be their sole customer exit partner

■Opportunities for Value Enhancement: Increasing funnel conversion (~$10MM incremental EBITDA per 1% increase, assuming constant 98% success rate) and monetizing customer data from 900k current/former timeshare owners

■Low Hanging Operational Improvement Opportunities: Castle Harlan believes WFG’s process can be streamlined, reducing 5-6 separate calls prior to engagement to 2-3

Financial Commentary

■Total Net Revenue: ~20% decline in 2022 revenues due to a 2-year lagged effect from the pandemic. In 2020, WFG experienced ~50% growth in revenue due to customers wanting to get out of their timeshares because of an inability to use them given Covid restrictions. When the pandemic subsided, WFG’s revenue declined due to an increase in timeshare sales/demand once restrictions were lifted and because very few timeshares had been sold in the preceding years

■Forecasted Revenue: Assumes average price per customer stays flat at ~$8.9K while Customers continue to grow YoY

■Refunds and Dropped Customers: Reflects the amount of lost revenue from customers who drop from the program and refunds in the event the process takes more than 36 months, bridging the difference of total contract value and net revenue

■Marketing: Assumes marketing spend to grow with the increase in customer acquisition. Company is able to maintain ~5.0x Rev/Ad. Spend per Acquired Customer (Facebook 5.4x, Google 3.8x, Radio 2.9x). Fully burdened for variable expenses, LTV/CAC is ~3.0x

■Historical Salaries & Wages and Sales Commission: Increase in 2019-2020 fueled by headcount growth to manage increased demand, followed by decrease in head count accompanying the decrease in demand

■Forecasted Salaries and Wages: Headcount is projected to stay flat through the projected period as wages gradually rise YoY

■Forecasted Sales Commission: Sales commission is held constant at ~7.5% of revenue, growing YoY as revenue does

■Other Opex: Grows as a percent of revenue YoY (assumes costs are 100% variable with no credit for operating leverage)

■EBITDA Adjustments: Primarily made up of Owners Compensation (2019: $13.9MM, 2020: 15.2MM, 2021:$4.5MM)

■Special Resolution Payment: $2.5MM paid out if each if the two lawsuits (Westgate and Diamond Resorts) are resolved by 12/31/2024 & 12/31/2026, respectively

■Earnout: $50MM Earnout payable over 4 years subject to a 25% IRR for the equity (10/12.5/12.5/15)
