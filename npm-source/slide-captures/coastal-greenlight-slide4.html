Initial Financial Analysis – Management Case

4

Commentary

■Revenue: Historical advertising revenue has been lumpy between years due to election cycles, and this lumpiness is projected in the forecast period. Retransmission revenue is projected to fall slightly over the forecast period due to a decline in subscribers. However, overall industry subscriber decline is expected to slow into 2028, and Coastal has also historically been able to increase retransmission fees over time

■Network Fees: Payments to affiliate networks as % of retransmission revenue

■Capex: The company has low capex, primarily consisting of technology investments, facilities, and construction/maintenance of towers. Once constructed, tower failure represents a very minimal risk, and TV towers have a lifespan of 30-50 years

■Amortization: Amortization is structured as to align with election cycles, allowing increased deleveraging during higher-revenue election years

■ECF Sweep: 90%, modeled quarterly
