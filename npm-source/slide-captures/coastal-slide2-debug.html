Industry Overview

■Pay TV Subscribers (including vMVPDs), have decreased at a 5.5% CAGR since 2018, and are expected to decrease at a 2.3% CAGR into 2028. However, broadcasting stations have been able to increase retransmission price per subscriber at an 5% CAGR since 2013 with a forecasted 4% CAGR into 2028. This drives a forecasted increase in retransmission fees from $15.2Bn to $15.6Bn in 2028 (~1% CAGR). TV advertising revenue is expected to fluctuate with political cycles but remain at ~$24.8Bn into 2028

■The Federal Communications Commission (“FCC”) regulations limit local station ownership beyond 1 in a Designated Market Area (“DMA”). Consolidation in the industry is possible through a sidecar, when service areas do not overlap, or one is not ranked top 4 in the DMA. The Trump administration plans extensive deregulation within the FCC, likely enabling further consolidation

Company Background

■Founded in 2008 and headquartered in Anchorage, Alaska, Coastal Television Broadcasting Group (“Coastal” or the “Company”) is a TV broadcaster that operates in 12 regional markets across the U.S.

•The Company is owned and operated by Bill Fielder, who purchased the Alaskan assets for $3.2MM from his former employer, Piedmont TV

■Coastal currently owns/operates 18 stations and 5 dual affiliated stations with the “Big 4” networks of Fox (11 stations), NBC (3), ABC (6), and CBS (3). The Company also has 7 shared-services agreements (“sidecars,” or separate entities associated with Coastal) predominantly in mid-sized markets across Alaska, Mississippi, Wyoming, New York, Arkansas, Indiana, North Dakota, and Minnesota

■The Company generates revenue through two segments:

•Retransmission (57% of 2024 Revenue): Payments from Multichannel Video Programming Distributors (“MVPDs”, includes cable companies and virtual MVPDs “vMVPDs” such as YouTubeTV, etc.) for station access. Coastal charges an average fee of $4.43/subscriber/month, earning a 33% margin after paying affiliate network fees (Fox, etc.). Affiliate and MVPD contracts are 2-3 years

•Advertising (43%): Advertising revenue consists of local advertisements (62%), political advertisements (27%), and national advertisements (10%). Advertising revenue is volatile between years due to the prevalence of political advertisements during election years

■The Company currently has an LOI in place with Imagicomm to purchase 7 of its TV stations for ($28.0MM, 2.9x Broadcast Cash Flow or “BCF”) in Syracuse, NY (Fox), Binghamton, NY (Fox), Alexandria, LA (ABC), Idaho Falls, ID (NBC), and Greenwood, MS (Fox/ABC, CBS, and NBC) (collectively, the “Target”)

•The acquisition provides an additional $44.8MM in revenue and $9.8MM of BCF in FY2024. Assets in the industry trade on BCF, which is EBITDA + Corporate G&A

•Local broadcast television programming is ~25% news/weather, ~15% prime network programming (including sports) and ~40% syndicated programming

■In FY24 Coastal generated PF revenue and PF Adj. EBITDA of $118.1MM and $28.1MM (24% margin)
